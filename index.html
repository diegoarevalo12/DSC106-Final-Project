The Relationship Between Temperature and Activity: Do Hotter Mice Move More?

By: Tommy Li, Khoi Phan, Diego Arevalo

Introduction

This interactive dashboard explores the relationship between body temperature and physical activity in mice. We analyze data from multiple mice of both sexes, looking at how temperature and activity levels fluctuate throughout a 24-hour cycle and whether there's a correlation between the two variables. We also investigate potential gender differences and the impact of factors like estrus periods in female mice.

Temperature & Activity Over Time

This visualization shows how temperature (red) and activity (blue) change over time. Notice the cyclical patterns and how temperature and activity often peak and decline together.

Correlation Between Temperature and Activity

Each point represents a moment in time. Points that form a trend from bottom-left to top-right indicate a positive correlation: as temperature increases, so does activity.

24-Hour Activity Pattern

This chart shows average activity levels throughout a 24-hour cycle, highlighting nocturnal patterns in mice behavior.

24-Hour Temperature Pattern

This chart shows average body temperature throughout a 24-hour cycle, allowing us to observe daily fluctuations.

Gender Comparison: Temperature vs Activity Correlation

This visualization compares how strongly temperature and activity are correlated in male and female mice. Bar height represents correlation strength (higher = stronger relationship).

Heatmap: Activity by Hour of Day

This heatmap shows activity patterns across different days and hours. Darker blue indicates higher activity levels.

Key Findings

Conclusion

Based on our analysis of mice temperature and activity data, we can draw several conclusions about the relationship between these variables and how they differ between genders. Our findings will be dynamically updated based on the interactive visualizations above.
